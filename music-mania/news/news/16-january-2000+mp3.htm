MP3

MP3, the file format that lets you dowwnload near CD quality audio very quickly, you can play the MP3 files on your computer or you can play them on a portable MP3 player. This is great for consumers, but not so great for the record labels and record shops, the labels are worried because it is possible to 'rip' an MP3 file from one of their CDs and post it on the net, the record shops are worried because the labels are offering free MP3 downloads on the net so consumers can sample new acts. David Bowie previewd his latest album, Hours, as a download, two weeks before it was released.
Web sites such as peoplesound.com invite unsigned musicians to post their music on the net, you can download some tracks and if you like them you can buy a CD. Stumble are four 19-year olds from Derby, their latest single 'How Many Times Do I Have to Kill You Before You Die?' has been played on Radio 1 and XFM, and it has been reviewd in NME and Melody Maker, yet you can only download it from peoplesound.com.
In October HMV launched hmv.co.uk, as well as selling CDs it offers free downloads. A new chain of stores called eGroove is about to be launched in America, using a computer you can select a CD you want to buy, and it will be burned instore within a few minutes!
