Madness formed in London in 1978, they were (in 1976) Mike Barson (keyboards), Lee 'Kix' Thomson (saxophone/vocals), Chris Foreman (guitar) and Chris Smash (aka Cathal Smyth / bass). A couple of years later (1978) Suggs (aka Graham McPherson / Vocals), Mark Bedford (bass) and Dan 'Woody' Woodgate (drums) joined the band, they made their debut in May 1979. The bands first single was called The Prince, it was dedicated to Prince Buster, when it reached number 16 in the UK Madness signed to Stiff Records. The Prince became the first in a line of 22 UK Top 20 singles. Their first album was called One Step Beyond it got to number seven and stayed in the British top 75 for nearly a year. In 1981 they made a film, Take It Or Leave It was about starting a group, they played themselves. The bands first British number one was House Of Fun, their only US Top 10 hit was Our House which got to number seven in 1983. In 1983 Mike Barson (keyboards) got fed up with the rock'n'roll lifestyle and retired. In 1988 Suggs, Lee Thomson, Chris Foreman and Chris Smash re-formed as The Madness, but a self-titled album was completely ignored. Suddenly in 1992, the group's greatest hits collection Divine Madness went to Number 1 in the UK, so in August 1992 Madness (including Mike Barson) reformed for two fantastic concerts in London's Finsbury Park. This year in 1999, they released their first single for over a decade, Lovestruck, they have also released an album, Wonderful and a box of their first six albums, The Lot. In December the band toured around England, they are a great live act.
