U2 are Bono (Paul Hewson), Adam Clayton, Larry Mullen and the Edge (David Evans). The huge success of the Joshua Tree and their show-stopping performance at Live Aid (1985) meant that by the late 1980's most people had an opinion on U2. In 1985 Rolling Stone described the band as 'The Only Band That Matters'. They had a huge soaring sound that was centered on Dave Evans (The Edge) reverb-laden guitar playing and Bono's great vocals. They singned to Island Records in 1980, their debut album was called Boy and it was produced by Steve Lillywhite. Their second LP was October, and October generated the singles I Will Follow and Gloria, both videos were heavily featured on MTV. The Unforgettable Fire was the first of several collaborations with producers Brian Eno and Daniel Lanois, The Unforgettable Fire got to number twelve in the US. The Joshua Tree spawned the number one hit I Still Haven't Found What I'm Looking For. The Joshua Tree was produced by Brian Eno and Daniel Lanois. The album won a Grammy in 1987 (Album Of The Year). In 1991 Achtung Baby reached Number 1, it featured funkier beats and metallic textures.
