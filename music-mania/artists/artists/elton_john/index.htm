Elton John was born in 1947, Reginald Kenneth Dwight. His rise to fame began in the 1960's when he took a job as a messenger at a music publishing company. He made money performing in bars, solo, or with his band Bluesology. In 1967 Elton John teamed up with lyricist Bernie Taupin, for most of the Seventies they were a virtual hit factory, with 25 singles in the Top Forty, on the 1980's they achieved over 36 Top 40 hits. Elton John was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1994. Reg Dwight auditioned for Liberty Records, the scouts liked his performance but were not so keen on his material, Bernie Taupin also audiotioned for Liberty, so one of the scouts gave Reg Dwight a bunch of Bernie Taupin's lyrics, six months later Reg Dwight (now calling himself Elton John) and Bernie Taupin met. They took their songs to music publisher, Dick James. Dick James owned all of Elton John & Bernie Taupin's compositions until 1975. Steve Brown, another music publisher, advised Elton John and Bernie Taupin to write rockier songs that Elton John could record. The first one of these was I've Been Loving You (1968). Elton John formed his own MCA - distributed label, he signed Kiki Dee, in 1976 Elton John and Kidi Dee recorded Don't Go Breaking My Heart. Caribou was released in 1974, it included Don't Let The Sun Go Down On Me. Elton John helped John Lennon back into the limelight, he played on John Lennon's comeback single, Whatever Gets You Thru The Night. In 1975 he had a hit with Someone Saved My Life Tonight from the album, Captain Fantastic. Also in 1975 Elton John was honoured with a star on Hollywood's Walk Of Fame. His first British Number 1 was Don't Go breaking My Heart (1976), a duet with Kiki Dee, also that year he had a Top 10 hit with Sorry Seems To Be The Hardest Word. In November 1977 Elton John announced he was retiring from performing. Bernie Taupin began collaberating with other artists. In the 80's he had hits including Sad Songs (Say So Much) and Nikita
